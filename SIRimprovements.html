More Staten Island Railway Trains


Updated 22 Aug 2024


Attribution: "R211S Cars Unveiled on Staten Island Railway - 53265773528" by MTAPhotos is licensed under CC BY 2.0 .


Staten Island needs improved and expanded MTA services to meet the growing demand for public transit. The Staten Island Railway operates at half-hour intervals outside rush hours, an inadequate service for a bustling community. With these extended wait times, Staten Islanders are faced with unnecessary inconvenience. By early 2025, the MTA is set to phase out all 58 of the 1970s-era R44 railcars and replace them with 75 innovative R211S railcars. These state-of-the-art railcars have accessibility, safety, space, information, and LED lighting enhancements. To fully realize the advantages, we strongly recommend that the MTA increases the frequency of the Staten Island Railway to a minimum of every 15 minutes throughout the day and early evening, with daily service starting as early as 6 AM and continuing until 9 PM or later. For a community as vibrant and active as Staten Island, the transit system must reflect its needs. Now is the time for the MTA to prioritize the Staten Island community and enhance Staten Island Railway operations.
