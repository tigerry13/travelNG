"Simplify" the Subway System


Updated 22 Aug 2024


Attribution: "MTA NYC Subway 🅠 train at 96th St" by Mtattrain is licensed under CC BY-SA 4.0 .


As a crucial component of our City’s infrastructure, the subway must continually adapt to the growing needs of its riders. We propose the following changes to improve service efficiency, enhance connectivity, and ensure that the subway is reliable for all New Yorkers.


Highly Recommended Changes:


  1. Divert 🅐 train operations by considering Far Rockaway-Mott Avenue (as always) and Rockaway Park-Beach 116th Street (not Ozone Park-Lefferts Boulevard) as the 🅐 line's southern terminals to enhance transit options near John F. Kennedy International Airport and the Rockaways.
  2. Extend the Lefferts Boulevard Shuttle from Euclid Avenue to Nostrand Avenue (with stops at Broadway Junction and Utica Avenue). Also, service should run 24/7 to offer seamless connectivity beneath Fulton Street.
  3. Ensure that 🅐 trains only run express in Manhattan, and that 🅑 and 🅓 trains make all local stops north of 59th Street-Columbus Circle.
  4. Evaluate and then potentially close the 50th Street 🅒🅔 subway station for better allocation of resources. Somewhere around this area, there should be track switches between downtown local and express tracks, and between uptown local and express tracks.
  5. Shift 🅑 trains onto the Washington Heights 🅐🅒 Line instead of the Concourse 🅑🅓 Line. If this happens, 🅑 train service has to be extended from weekdays to daily, that way 🅐 trains can run express in Manhattan during daytime and evening hours. Otherwise, 🅐 trains would have to make all local stops whenever 🅑 trains do not run.
  6. Swap routes such that 🅓 trains serve DeKalb Avenue and the Brighton 🅑🅠 Line, while 🅠 trains utilize the 4th Avenue 🅓🅝🅡 and West End 🅓 Lines for improved service consistency.
  7. Streamline other subway services by ending operations of the 🅒🅦 and Rockaway Park Shuttle trains.
  8. Redirect 🅡 trains to run on the Astoria 🅝🅦 Line rather than the Queens Boulevard 🅔🅕🅜🅡 Line to enhance train utilization.
  9. Reroute 🅝 trains onto the 🅠 line north of Canal Street, optimizing service patterns and connections.
  10. Run more 🅐🅔 and 🅡 trains during the daytime and early evening, especially on the weekends, to accommodate rising ridership demands.
  11. Enhance subway access to a critical area of Queens by restoring 🅖 train service to and from Forest Hills-71st Avenue.
  12. Enable all 🅕 trains to run express between Jay Street-MetroTech and Church Avenue to reduce 🅕 and 🅖 train delays. If this happens, lower-level platforms at the Bergen Street 🅕🅖 subway station must reopen.
  13. Realign 🅕 trains to operate beneath 53rd Street instead of 63rd Street and 🅜 trains to operate beneath 63rd Street instead of 53rd Street, accommodating better travel flow and connections. If this happens, lengthened 🅜 train service has to be extended from weekdays to 24/7.
  14. Replace the 🅙/🅩 skip-stop pattern with extra rush-hour 🅙 trains to lessen confusion.

Benefits of These Changes:


  1. Provide more transit options by improving access to key destinations.
  2. Reduce confusion and delays by streamlining operations through clearer routing and service patterns.
  3. Increase subway service by enhancing overall capacity and ensure timely arrivals and departures.
  4. Solve subway interlining issues by addressing many chronic service conflicts, especially those in Downtown Brooklyn.
  5. Maximize CBTC technology by improving its effectiveness and reliability.

In May 2023, the MTA announced phased subway service enhancements that kicked off in July 2023 by running more ➊➌➎➏🅑🅒🅓🅖🅙🅜🅝 and 🅡 trains. They focused on improving service during high ridership periods to align service with post-COVID ridership trends and provide more efficient travel experiences for New Yorkers. It would be ideal to run the ➐ and 🅛 trains every 10 (not 20) minutes overnight, as they do not connect with other subway lines. If this concept also applies to the ➊ and ➏ lines, overnight ➋ and ➍ trains could then run express in Manhattan; the MTA could also run full-time ➌ train service in Manhattan and Brooklyn.
